Plexus: How does data fit in?

Sometimes the public-facing sources of health care data – like NHS Choices and mental health charity Mind’s own website – are not fully accurate: contact or service information might be out-of-date and Local Minds might be listed but no longer active.

Through working with Mind on its knowledge platform, OpenHub, for the past 18 months, M/A saw the potential richness of the data being collected. With the use of core information from the 146 Local Minds on OpenHub, M/A wanted to produce a clear picture of the support Mind offers across England and Wales.

M/A’s open web app Plexus uses a combination of data from APIs and websites, including data on:

  • health care services, from Mind’s OpenHub platform and NHS Choices
  • employee rights and responsibilities from GOV.UK, the Department for Work & Pensions and Citizens Advice
  • labour market and population data from Nomis
  • health and deprivation from Office for National Statistics
  • job opportunities and advice from National Careers Service, Employee volunteering, Time to Change, Timebank UK

For more information on how the data was gathered, see the methodology section.

By combining all this data into an application, Plexus can be used by people with mental health conditions, their families and employers to:

  • find Mind and NHS support services in their local areas, over multiple distances
  • find routes into volunteering, part-time or full-time employment
  • understand how to demonstrate skills and experience in the best way
  • navigate job applications and meet their requirements
  • understand employer’s responsibilities to those recently signed off or returning to work from a period of absence
  • find legal information and points of contact for legal support

Plexus will soon be developed to expand on the type and diversity of the datasets it uses and create new information threads and streams within all data currently collated in OpenHub. Plexus will also explore the potential for analysing its user data to gain new insights.

We would like to investigate the data we are able to collect from our users’ interactions and see if this can be cross-referenced with the other datasets to give social, economic and geographic insights. [These] could be taken forward and married into new and anonymised datasets, which could be certified and released as open data.

– Martin Vowles, Director of M/A
