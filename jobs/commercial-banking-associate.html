Investment banking associate

As part of the ODI’s focus on data infrastructure, we are exploring how making it open can bring new value to the investment banking sector and its clients.

We are collaborating with Deutsche Bank to understand how open innovation and emerging technologies can benefit the sector.

Who are we looking for?

The ODI are looking for someone who knows the investment banking sector inside out, with an interest in policy and an ability to write clearly and engagingly for a wide audience. Your background might be in banking, law or financial regulation. We do not need you to have a background in data or in technology

What would the work involve?

This is a freelance position, with a likely total commitment of approximately 10 days during October and November, 2016. You will be responsible for working independently, guided by ODI colleagues to:

  • Carry out research into existing data infrastructure, its challenges and opportunities in the investment banking sector

  • Write a blog post that summarises this research and helps members of the investment banking sector to think differently about data and it uses

  • Create a first draft of a short report about the data infrastructure needed for the investment banking sector, and recommendations for how the sector can make it as open as possible to create new value.

We offer flexible working but you will need to be available to come into our London offices for at least five of the total of 10 days.

Day rate is between £350 and £500 per day, depending on experience.

About the team

• Teamwork. We are all here to work towards achieving the same aims and mission of the ODI and so all team members will be required to work together across departments and with colleagues to ensure the long term success and sustainability of the ODI.

• Agility. To meet the changing demands of a rapidly growing organisation we will need to be agile to meet shifting challenges and workloads. Team members will be required to take on or let go of tasks and responsibilities either for the short or long term that will be both reasonable and within their capabilities.

• Respect. The ODI expects everyone to respect and comply with the principles of dignity at work and equal opportunities for everyone.

• Confidentiality. We are all about open data but what comes with that is the need to be mindful of people’s personal data, intellectual property and any business sensitive data. If in any doubt please always ask first before publishing or passing on any data.

• Health and safety. Please ensure that you cooperate and comply with health and safety obligations, particularly by reporting promptly any defects, risks or potential hazards.

How to apply

Please send a CV, a writing sample and no more than 300 words explaining why you are the right person for the role to [email protected] by midnight on Monday 10 October 2016.
