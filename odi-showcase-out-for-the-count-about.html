Out for the Count: About

Out for the Count is a new campaign calling for more open and accessible data on local elections across the UK.

The lack of an accessible platform with elections, candidate and results information has made it hard for UK voters to understand whom they are electing in local elections.

With funding and support from the ODI, Out for the Count delivered the UK’s first election results tracker for the local government elections on 5 May 2016. This tracker crowdsourced results live from counts taking place across the country as they came in, with the help of volunteer ‘Count Correspondents’.

Out for the Count is a project run in partnership between the Local Government Information Unit (LGiU) and Democracy Club.

Both the LGiU and Democracy Club are dedicated to improving democracy. The LGiU covers local elections as part of its wider local government work. Democracy Club helps open up data to make better digital services for citizens.

Out for the Count’s vision is to help speed up the feedback loop for voters, so if they vote, they can understand the impact their vote had. This helps to support the broader aim of ensuring greater transparency in local elections and more-informed choices for voters.

The crowdsourced database of results that was collected by the team and volunteer Count Correspondents is now accessible as a full list of the results and an interactive map, hosted by Democracy Club.

In support of open elections around the world, the Out for the Count results tracker can be used again for elections everywhere. The Out for the Count team plans to further refine and use the model they have developed during by-elections over the next year, and for the local elections in 2017.
