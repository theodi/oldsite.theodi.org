Summit 2016 Training Day

What is your open data return on investment?

Monday 31 October 2016, 10:00am - 10:00am

Arup, 13 Fitzroy St, London, W1T 4BQ

We're told that open data creates social, economic and environmental impact. But what does it mean for your organisation? And is it worth the effort? This session seeks to unpick what open data ROI is, where it's worked, and to share experiences about open data ROI.

Suitable for

Anyone who has used open data in their organisation, or is thinking about implementing it into their organisational strategy.

About the trainer: Edafe Onerhime

Edafe Onerhime qualified as an Open Data Institute certified trainer in September 2014 having completed an MSc in Business Intelligence focused on health open data, where her dissertation won the Dean's award for postgraduate study.

Edafe advises and supports governments, businesses, civil societies and the third sector, at Open Data Services. The digital co-operative incubates and supports open data standards, including Open Contracting and 360Giving; two standards adopted by the UK government at the May 2016 anti-corruption summit and in the UK National Action Plan.

As a data geek, Edafe champions digital literacy, diversity and inclusiveness, particularly for the third sector, women in technology and minorities. Through her consultancy practice, she hosts data clinics to provide free advice on making sense of data, mentors people interested in improving their data skills and trains a diverse audience in open data and data literacy. She is a regular speaker at events with a focus on bringing tech to the people and demystifying data.

Trainer

Edafe Onerhime

Edafe joined ODI Leeds in September, 2014 as an ODI Associate and ODI Registered Trainer. Her focus is on growing and developing the training offered by the Node in close collaboration with the its associates, members and attendees.

Edafe specialises in data strategy: helping organisations get the most out of their data. Her experience covers the full data life-cycle from acquisition to analysis, modelling and processing to visualisation.

Edafe holds an MSc in Business Intelligence, which involved a dissertation research project on open health data. This was when she discovered her passion for all things 'open'.

Before joining the ODI, Edafe worked to make the most of data in diverse industries in the public and private sectors, including Stepchange debt charity, Dabs.com (an e-tailer) and Premex (a medico-legal firm).

Edafe is an advocate for open knowledge, open data, digital literacy, peer-to-peer learning and women in Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (STEM).

Edafe loves to travel; the more unusual the place and pace, the better. Her dream holiday is a grand tour of Europe by train.
