Open Data Leaders Network: an open call for outstanding government leaders

The Open Data Leaders Network (ODLN) programme calls for exceptional global leaders of open data initiatives worldwide, to take part in its 6-month programme in 2016-17

The ODLN is a unique global network of 30 open data leaders. Through tested peer-to-peer methodology, the programme aims to provide the necessary knowledge and tools for effective open data leadership, and to give leaders access to a network of outstanding peers to share ideas and support over time.

The programme hopes to sustain lasting relationships among open data leaders. Participants will take part in a programme that blends carefully selected face-to-face and online engagements that finetune their leadership and change management skills, and helps channel their training into open data initiatives at home.

Launched in February 2015, the programme started with 14 participants from countries including the UK, Chile, Morocco and Macedonia.

Despite living in different countries, ODLN participants often share similar roadblocks and opportunities in their government work. More than a network of committed leaders, it has become a community of friends, according to Paul Stone, New Zealand’s Open Government Data programme leader and a former participant. “Friends who share the same passion about open data, and who are striving to impact the culture of the governments we work for.”

This year, the ODI will award a selection of fully and partially funded places for outstanding government leaders. Selected leaders will have a clear record of successful leadership and involvement in the implementation of national, regional or city open data initiatives.

For more information about the ODLN, watch our short film and read a blog written by one of our former participants.

What we offer

Through its 10 places for government leaders involved in open data, the ODLN offers:

  • A one-week, peer-to-peer, stimulating programme in London, between 5 September and 9 September, 2016
  • The opportunity to apply for a mini-grant valued at up to £6,500 to enhance an open data initiative
  • Executive coaching from relevant ODI experts
  • Monthly online meetups for the duration of the programme with ODI specialists, co-led with ODLN members for a period of up to six months
  • A platform to showcase work at international events, such as the International Open Data Conference and the ODI Summit

What we look for

Strong applicants for the ODLN programme will:

  • Hold a key position within government, having demonstrated experience in implementing a municipal, regional or national open data agenda
  • Think creatively and share innovative ideas about the future of open data in their respective cities, regions or countries
  • Be prepared to gain from peer-to-peer knowledge and idea sharing
  • Demonstrate commitment to joining a high-impact leadership programme, over a sustained period of time
  • Demonstrate their willingness to transform the lessons learned through the ODLN into positive change for their respective governments and communities

Funding

  • Partially funded places will be available for applicants from the European Union and high-income economies (the ODI will cover programme costs only)
  • Fully funded places will be available for applicants from least developed countries low-income countries, lower-middle-income countries and upper-middle-income countries (the ODI will cover programme costs, travel and accommodation).

Key dates:

  • The application process closes on 20 July, 2016 at 17:00 GMT
  • Successful applicants will be notified by 27 July, 2016
  • Selected participants will be invited to attend the Open Data Leaders Network from 5 September to 9 September, 2016 at the ODI’s offices in London

How to apply:

Interested leaders can download the application form here. Please read our terms and conditions before applying.

The full list of judging criteria is available on request. If you would like more information, or have any questions, please contact [email protected].

The OD4D programme is managed by Canada’s International Development Research Centre (IDRC). It is a donor partnership with the World Bank, the United Kingdom’s Department for International Development (DfID), and Canada's Department of Foreign Affairs, Trade, and Development (DFATD).

The Open Data Leaders Network is also supported by the European Data Portal, which is being developed by the European Commission with the support of a consortium led by Capgemini, including INTRASOFT International, Fraunhofer Fokus, Con terra, Sogeti, the Open Data Institute, Time.Lex and the University of Southampton.
