Announcing the 2015 Open Data Awards finalists

Earlier this year we opened nominations to everyone for our second annual Open Data Awards. We are delighted now to announce the finalists who will each be invited to our awards evening on the 9 July.

The finalists come from across the world and represent just a few of the issues where open data is making a difference - from malaria and democracy to the internet of things and finance.

As you'll see from the shortlist and original nominations, the judges had an incredibly difficult job deciding on our final few. There are some amazing open data stories to be found across all the nominations, and we would encourage you to take time to look through.

The winners will be announced at an evening event in London on the 9 July by Tim Berners Lee and Nigel Shadbolt.

The 2015 finalists

Business award - celebrating open data used to transform business

  • Oxford Consultants for Social Inclusion - @ocsi_uk
  • Open Corporates - @OpenCorporates
  • Open Data Nation - @OpenDataNation

Innovation award - celebrating open data used as a tool for innovation, in any sector

  • A Tu Servicio (At Your Service) - @DATAuy
  • Medicines for Malaria Venture - @MedsforMalaria
  • OpenSensors.io - @opensensorsio

Social Impact award - celebrating open data used as a tool for social good

  • BudgIT - @budgitng
  • Democracy Club - @democlub
  • Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team - @hotosm

Individual Champion award – celebrating an outstanding individual pioneer of open data

  • Denis Gursky - @denis_gursky
  • Linet Kwamboka - @g33kmate
  • Mo McRoberts - @nevali

Publisher award - celebrating high publishing standards and use of challenging data

  • Greater London Authority Data Store - @LDN_data
  • Kenya Open Data Initiative - @KenyaOpenData
  • Open Corporates - @OpenCorporates
