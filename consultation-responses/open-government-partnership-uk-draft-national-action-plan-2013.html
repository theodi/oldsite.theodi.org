Consultation Responses

Open Government Partnership: UK draft National Action Plan 2013

The Open Government Partnership (OGP) UK National Action Plan arises from OGP activities and the requirements for a National Action Plan that arise from OGP. The G8 Open Data Charter also requires a National Action Plan, and we are assuming that this National Action Plan is intended to address those commitments as well as those from OGP.

Section 1 of the National Action Plan focuses on Open Data and Transparency. It is not clear if the definition of “open data” that is used within this draft is the same as that defined in the Open Data White Paper, namely data that is accessible, available in a machine-readable format, and available under an open licence. The emphasis in the draft is on access to information, not on the ability to reuse it.

We would like the National Action Plan to use the same definition of “open data” as is used in the Open Data White Paper, and to ensure that it is clear when a commitment is about providing basic access to information or enabling reuse of data.

We are encouraged to see open data being used as policy tool in an increasingly wide and diverse range of areas. Our main priority for open data for the UK is establishing the National Information Infrastructure, as an essential core capability. We believe other OGP members will also benefit from taking an infrastructural view of open data.

We broadly agree with the commitments made in Section 1 which highlight the way in which open data can be used to facilitate and support transparency, but feel they should go further in detailing the actual commitment made, timescales and responsibilities. For example, we would like to see commitments:

  • to engage with the OpenContracting project to develop standards for publishing contracts as open data, and for GDS to publish government contracts through Contract Finder against those standards by October 2015
  • for The National Archives to publish through its Digital Records Infrastructure, as open data, the catalogue metadata about newly released historical government records as part of the transition from 30 to 20 years, as these are made available, and for previously released records by October 2015
  • for the Register of Lobbyists to be published as open data from its first release

In general, wherever there is an activity oriented around making government more transparent and accountable, there is benefit in identifying datasets that, through their release as open data, could support that activity. We would like the UK National Action Plan to contain:

  • clear stated commitments for releasing new open data, improving existing open data, or embedding open data publication into existing processes
  • a commitment to issuing procurement guidance that embeds open data publication and reuse into newly contracted services
  • a commitment to measuring the quality and quantity of open data that is released, for which we recommend the use of the ODI Open Data Certificates
  • a commitment to review progress against those measures on a regular basis

Given that the Transparency Team is leading the definition of the National Information Infrastructure, we think there is an opportunity for the National Action Plan to set out the success criteria for that activity. For ODI, the success of the initiative is whether the plan for National Information Infrastructure contains:

  • some designated datasets which contain identifiers and reference data for core national infrastructure such as schools and hospitals (see below)
  • an indication for each of these datasets about the current level of publication against the ODI’s Open Data Certificate
  • a realistic plan for each dataset for their eventual publication as Expert-level open data against the ODI’s Open Data Certificate including: - quantifying benefits (such as efficiency savings) to the wider public sector
  • a business case for sustainable publication linked to those benefits
  • a definition of the technical approach to publication, including which open standard formats will be used, and how identifiers will be resolved
  • an engagement plan that ensures the data gets maximum used and is improved over time
  • dated milestones towards an Expert-level open data release
  • a process for embedding the publication of National Information Infrastructure datasets as part of the core public task of the organisations concerned
  • a process by which other National Information Infrastructure datasets may be identified over time
  • a set of metrics for National Information Infrastructure, and a plan for how these are measured and reviewed over time

To be consistent with the G8 Open Data Charter, we will be looking for datasets in the categories identified within that work, such as:

  • Companies - registered companies, VAT registered companies, registered charities, non-domestic buildings
  • Crime and Justice - police forces, police stations, crimes (ie Home Office classification), prisons, courts, judges & magistrates, case law
  • Earth observation - weather observation points, farms, fields, forests, river network, lakes, protected areas
  • Education - schools & academies, higher education providers, nurseries, examination boards, qualifications
  • Energy and Environment - pollution measuring points, flood zones, power stations & substations, power network
  • Finance and contracts - public sector bodies, contracts (framework and otherwise), spending categories
  • Geospatial - maps, addresses
  • Global Development - cadastral parcels, mines, oil platforms
  • Government Accountability and Democracy - parliaments, parliamentary seats, wards, polling stations, political parties, legislation, government departments & arms-length bodies, civil service positions, local authorities
  • Health - hospitals, GP practices, dental surgeries, other health providers, treatments, medicines, illnesses
  • Science and Research - research institutes, research councils
  • Statistics - census regions (ie output areas)
  • Social mobility and welfare - social housing, benefits, taxes, job centres
  • Transport and Infrastructure - railways & stations, bus stops, airports, ferry terminals, roads, driving test centres, vehicles, rail operators

These are important core datasets into which other datasets can reference for additional context. We note that some of these datasets may require work with local authorities to ensure that data is published in a way that enables easy aggregation.

We hope that this early feedback helps you to publish another draft shortly for further iterative review and refinement.
